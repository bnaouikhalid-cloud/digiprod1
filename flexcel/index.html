Review My Data & Continuity Questions

Objection removal

Everything your team will ask before the first workflow goes live.

Will my team use it?
The employee experience is designed around familiar Excel and WhatsApp inputs. Adoption still depends on good mapping and calibration, which is why the rollout begins with the real team workflow.
Do we need to abandon Excel?
No. Flexcel’s current positioning is to automate around existing sheets. The exact workbook, Office requirements and data design are confirmed during mapping.
Can it connect to Tally?
Tally connection is a current Flexcel capability. Direction, frequency and fields vary by use case, so the integration scope should be confirmed rather than assuming every flow is bidirectional.
How long does implementation take?
The live site illustrates a short, staged implementation for suitable sheet-automation scopes. A responsible timeline follows workflow mapping, data review, access requirements and edge-case calibration.
Who owns the data?
The current DigiProd site states that the client owns its data. Hosting, permissions, backup, retention and export responsibilities should be made explicit in the signed engagement.
What happens when our process changes?
Changes are reviewed against historical data and the connected logic. Flexcel is modular, so agreed refinements and new modules can be planned without replacing the whole flow.
Will we depend on one developer?
DigiProd’s existing continuity message describes shared documentation, code review and developer shadowing. Ask for the project-specific handover, support and access model in writing.
Can we start with one workflow?
Yes. The current Flexcel page explicitly recommends starting with the narrowest bottleneck and expanding as the team gains confidence.
